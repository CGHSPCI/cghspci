patient rigths and responsibilities
PATIENT'S RIGHTS

As valued patient of Comunity General Hospital, he/she is entitled to:

  • Considerate and respectful care and treatment in a clean and safe environment free from restrains and discrimination regardless of race, color, creed, sex, disability or sexual orientation.
  • Emergency care when needed.
  • Information on diagnosis, treatment and prognosis written on layman's terms.
  • Decisions on treatment procedures taken before discharge from the hospital.
  • Consent obtained before any non-emergency procedure begins.
  • Information on management of pain reliefs
  • Identify of health care staff involved in care service.
  • Refusal to further treatment examination or management by attending health care provider. Consequences of treatment refusal or of replacing a doctor or staff member shall explain be explained. (Note. To sign waiver on treatment refusal or change of attending staff.)
  • Medical abstract for a reasonable fee; true copies of laboratory examination results and other diagnostic procedures for legal purposes only
  • Written discharge plan and description of medications
  • Privacy while in the hospital
  • Itemized billings
  • Room visit of family members and well-wishers subject to prescribed visitation hours.
  • Suggestions or recommendations about the health care service of CGHSPCI

    • PATIENT'S RESPONSIBILITY

    As valued patient of Comunity General Hospital, you and/or your representativeare expected to:

  • Provide complete and accurate information about his/her health, including present condition if suffering from any communicable/infectious disease, past illnesses and hospitalization, medications, allergies, spcecial diet, ingestion of natural products and vitamins, and any other matter that pertains to his/her health.
  • Provide complete and accurate information including full name, address, home telephone number, date of birth, insurance carrier, and employer. To present valid ID (SSS/GSIS, voter's ID, passport, postal, PRC or driver's license); in case covered with benefits under HMO/Insurance Broker's or Company, health card identification is also required for inclusion ti CGHSPCI database.
  • Provide Hospital with the list of directives for your health care management during the period of confinement. (Note. ICU patients carry over; madical cases, CLOSED door policy is in effect)
  • Inform the doctor of possible problems in following precribed treatment or alernative therapies are preferred.
  • Tell the doctor or nurse about apprehensions in taking pain medication.
  • Leave valuables at home and bring only those items needed during hospital stay. The management is not liable for any loss or damage during stay.
  • Abide by all hospital rules and regulations
  • Comply with the NO SMOKING policy.
  • Comply with Visiting Hours and Policities to protect the rights and comfort of other patients. Be considerate of noise levels, privacy and safety. Deadly weapons are strickly prohibited inside hospital premises.
  • Treat hospital staff, other patients, and visitors with courtesy respect.
  • Provide complete and accurate information on insurance claims and work with the hospital HMO's Officer to ensure proper payment settlement
  • Present health insurance coverage with names of accredited hospitals and doctors. Present LOA reimbursement
  • Settle bills promptly.

    • NO SMOKING

    The CGHSPCI prescribes a strict NO Smoking policy for patients, staff and visitors anywhere within its premises. The includes the hospital entrances, courtyards and outdoor areas.

    If you are interested in quit smoking, you can contact our Cardiology specialists for details on Smoking Cessation Program.
